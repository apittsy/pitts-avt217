Who They Are

Bats are a misunderstood species!

They are excellent pollinators, hunt insect pests, and help make our forests grow. There are over 1,000 species of bats, yet they are vulnerable to endangerment.

Bats take on many different jobs!

Plant and seed pollinators help nature grow and thrive. The insect hunters can save farmers billions of dollars per year by eating.

Endangerment

Bats are losing their homes due to deforestation and human disturbance.

Manmade wind turbines are also affecting bats. A harmful disease is killing much of the bat population in North America.

Bat Facts!

A single little brown bat can catch over 1,000 insects in just one hour!

Bats account for about 20% of the entire mammal population.

Vampire bats adopt orphaned bats, and share food and shelter them.
